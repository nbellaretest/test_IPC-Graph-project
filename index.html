About the IPC Community of Practice and webinars
Launched March 2022

The Infection Prevention and Control (IPC) Community of Practice links national, sub national and facility level teams that are responsible for IPC programmes. Through interactive ninety minute online sessions it supports colleagues to share practical experience, learn from peers and translate IPC guidance into action in health facilities.

Sessions are led by WHO headquarters together with regional offices and partner institutions. Each session usually includes two or three country case studies, polling questions and a moderated panel discussion. Materials, tools and recordings are shared on the IPC Global Community of Practice platform to support continuous learning.

Example webinar agenda
Turning COVID 19 into a learning opportunity

The example below presents the structure of a ninety minute IPC Community of Practice session on sustaining IPC achievements from the COVID 19 response. It illustrates the balance of evidence updates, country perspectives and policy discussion.

Title
Maintaining infection prevention and control achievements from the COVID 19 response

Format
Ninety minute virtual session with interpretation in Arabic, French and Portuguese.

Session objectives

  • Review key IPC lessons from the COVID 19 pandemic.
  • Discuss how to maintain and institutionalise IPC gains in health facilities.
  • Highlight country experience, challenges and practical approaches.

Staying connected

The IPC Community of Practice continues to expand the range of topics and regions represented. New sessions will focus on practical implementation challenges, monitoring and evaluation of IPC programmes and the integration of IPC with broader quality of care initiatives.

For further information, potential collaboration or to share country experience suggestions for upcoming sessions, colleagues can reach the coordination team through the IPC Global Community of Practice platform.
